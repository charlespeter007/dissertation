How Criminal Defence Solicitors Sustain Ethical and Professional Agency under Structural, Institutional, and Emotional Pressures

This research explores how criminal defence solicitors in England and Wales sustain ethical and professional agency in the realities of practice: constrained resources, institutional demands, and the emotional weight of criminal defence work. The study seeks to produce a rigorous, practice-relevant account of how solicitors maintain standards, judgment, and professional identity under pressure—without treating resilience as a simple personal trait.

Research objectives

  • Describe the pressures shaping criminal defence practice (structural, institutional, and emotional).
  • Explore how solicitors sustain ethical and professional agency, including how standards are maintained in difficult conditions.
  • Identify common strategies used to preserve professional judgment, identity, and wellbeing without reducing these issues to individual “toughness”.
  • Produce findings useful to the profession: pressures, protective practices, and points where agency is strained.
